✍️ Humanized Case Study (UPI Fraud)

UPI fraud has become very common in recent times, especially among students and young users who frequently use online payment apps. In this type of fraud, scammers try to trick people into sending money or sharing sensitive details like OTPs or UPI PINs.

One common situation is when a student is selling something online, and a fake buyer shows interest. The scammer sends a QR code or a payment request and tells the student to scan it to receive money. Many people don’t realize that scanning a QR code actually sends money instead of receiving it. As a result, the victim ends up losing money.

Another way this fraud happens is through fake messages or calls pretending to be from banks or payment apps. These messages ask users to click on links and enter their details. Once the user enters their information, the scammer gains access to their account and can transfer money without permission.

Students are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such tricks. These frauds can lead to financial loss and stress. This shows how important it is to stay alert and use digital payment systems carefully.
